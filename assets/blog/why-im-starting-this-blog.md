# Why I'm Starting This Blog

**March 17, 2026**

Teaching has always been something running in the background of my career, studies and life. At university, I led **PASS** — Peer Assisted Study Sessions — where I helped younger students work through projects I'd done the year(s) before. After that, I picked up online tutoring - at the time I needed some extra cash, but I enjoyed the process of teaching, so I carried on next to my career.

I recently stopped tutoring again due to the time and energy it takes. But I thought, with the advent of AI, I have an opportunity to more easily share some of those lessons as well as some other experiences I've had in my career.

## What This Blog Is For

This blog will (hopefully) be the culmination of my experience as a developer. It's easy to forget how much you know and I hope that this will help me somewhat to build some kind of path of knowledge (both for me and others). 

I will probably have a focus on how to leverage AI to do a lot of these things as well.

## What I'll Be Writing About

The topics will broadly fall into a few areas:

- **Foundations** — The things that matter regardless of platform or language: clean code, architecture principles, testing
- **Android & Flutter** — Native Android with Kotlin and Jetpack Compose, and cross-platform development with Flutter and Dart
- **Career & Experience** — Reflections on working in the industry, things I've got wrong, things I'd do differently
- **Personal Projects** — Deep dives into the apps and tools I build in my own time, and the decisions behind them

More posts coming soon.
